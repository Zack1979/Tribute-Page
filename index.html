Key Highlights of Mahmoud Guinia's Life and Works

  • Guinia was born in the city Essaouira on the Atlantic coast-Morocco.
  • Moroccan music label Fikriphone released records of both live Lila ceremonies and studio sessions.
  • The release of Tichkaphone, whose materials were distributed in France by Sonodisc, and Agadir's La Voix El Maarif.
  • The Black Mluks was recorded and produced by Eric Rosenzveig in Montreal at Radio Canada and released on P-Vine Records in Japan.
  • The most famous western release was co-produced by Bill Laswell and Eric Rosenzveig and featured the American saxophone player Pharoah Sanders along with family members of Mahmoud's ensemble including his brothers Mohktar and Abdellah.
  • Another release, with Peter Brötzmann and Hamid Drake, was recorded at Wels's 1996 Music Unlimited festival.
  • The Shaman of the Sahara music recordings were released with Maâllem Mahmoud Guinia in collaboration with Tata Guines, Victor Vidal Paz and various Indonesian musicians.
  • Final studio recordings at Plein Les Oreilles studios in Casablanca with producer Ali Faraoui.
  • Guinia died after a lengthy illness.
  • Release of Mahmoud guinia's final studio recordings on double vinyl by UK record label, Hive Mind Records.
